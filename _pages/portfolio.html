  • CS 583 (Natural Language Processing) - Symptom Extraction and Linking from Vaccine Adverse Event Reports
    Relevant Skills: Natural Language Processing, Machine Learning, Word Embedding Models, Prompt Engineering, Data Handling and Preprocessing, Statistical Analysis, Data Visualization
    In the NLP-Based Symptom Detection and Entity Linking in VAERS Adverse Events project, I developed an automated Natural Language Processing (NLP) system for detecting and linking symptoms from 1 million VAERS (Vaccine Adverse Event Reporting System) reports. Utilizing Named Entity Recognition (NER) and sequence labeling techniques, the system identified adverse event symptoms and linked them to standardized terms. Various word embedding models—Word2Vec, GloVe, and FastText—were employed to evaluate symptom similarity, achieving accuracy rates of 19.11%, 14.12%, and 29.78%, respectively, despite challenges posed by unreliable ground truth generated from Large Language Models (LLMs). Statistical analysis was performed on 100 unique common symptoms, categorized by demographics such as state, age, and gender.

  • CS 541 (Artificial Intelligence) - Optimizing NBA Shot Performance: Clustering and Efficiency Analysis
    Relevant Skills: Machine Learning, Artificial Intelligence, Data Visualization, Data Analysis, Feature Engineering, Statistical Analysis, Empirical Evaluation, Clustering techniques, Rank Evaluation
    In the Optimizing NBA Performance Through Shot Data Clustering and Custom Efficiency project, I led a team of four to analyze NBA shot logs and develop a custom metric to evaluate Player Offensive Efficiency (POE). Using feature engineering, we identified critical factors affecting shooting efficiency and applied K-Means clustering to group similar shot contexts. We visualized shot patterns and compared our POE rankings with ESPN rankings, achieving a 58.09% Rank Biased Overlap, validating the effectiveness of the custom metric. The project involved cleaning data, engineering features, and conducting statistical analyses to provide actionable insights into player performance.

    In the High-Precision Handwritten Math Equation Recognition with Neural Networks project, I led a team of three to design a CNN-LSTM-based model for recognizing handwritten mathematical equations.
