      <a href="{{ site.baseurl }}/files/CS524-Course Project.pdf" target="_blank">Building Microservices and a CI/CD</a> <br> This project focused on transforming a monolithic application into microservices using an AWS CI/CD pipeline. It involved designing and implementing a microservices architecture with AWS Cloud9, ECS (Elastic Container Service), ECR (Elastic Container Registry), CodeCommit, and CodePipeline. The objective was to improve scalability by breaking down the monolithic structure into modular microservices. Key tasks included building AWS infrastructure, deploying Docker containers, configuring Load Balancers for efficient traffic management, and utilizing ECS with CodeDeploy for optimized deployment processes. The project aimed to enhance deployment efficiency and reliability through automated CI/CD practices tailored for microservices architecture. 
    </li>
    <li>
      <a href="{{ site.baseurl }}/files/CS584_FinalReport.pdf" target="_blank">Symptom Extraction and Linking from Vaccine Adverse Event Reports</a> <br> This research paper focuses on automating the identification of adverse events and symptoms in Vaccine Adverse Event Reports (VAERS) through advanced computational techniques. By leveraging Named Entity Recognition (NER) for symptom entity extraction and employing state-of-the-art entity linking methods, the study aims to link extracted symptoms to standardized terms. The project utilizes innovative approaches from recent literature, including optimized NER models for biomedical text and advanced clinical embeddings for entity linking. Despite challenges such as handling unstructured language in VAERS reports and the absence of ground truth data, the research contributes significantly to enhancing healthcare safety protocols and lays a foundation for future advancements in adverse event detection and management.
    </li>
    <li>
      <a href="{{ site.baseurl }}/files/Group 7 - Agile Article.pdf" target="_blank">Agile and Remote Work: Navigating the Future of Distributed Teams</a> <br> This research article delves into the intricate intersection of Agile methodologies and remote work dynamics, emphasizing their synergistic potential in navigating the challenges of distributed teams effectively. It explores Agile principles such as sprints, scrum, and kanban, which promote iterative development and collaborative frameworks. The study highlights the adaptation of Agile practices to remote environments, focusing on communication strategies, tool selection, and organizational structures. By investigating these themes, the paper aims to provide insights into enhancing productivity, innovation, and employee satisfaction in the context of increasing virtual collaboration.
